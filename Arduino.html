Introduction

This paper talks about a project that i worked on that uses arduino to controll an electro magnet that makes another magnet float.
For school we had to make something to do with electronics or robotics and i had seen lots of people make this before so i thought it would be worth a shot.

I had to make mine a tiny bit different from the people on youtubes because i had different parts to use.

Method

Making the Magnet

To make the magnet i had to wrap copper wire neatly and tightly around a nail, and after every wind of copper wire i would have to tape it with electrcal tape to get the most efficency that i could get. This works because having electricity flowing through a wire makes a weak magnetic field. When wrapped wrapped around a nail all the wires magnetic field stacks up like a bar magnet, however this is stil not very strong so you wrap it around a ferromagnetic material like a nail to make it stronger. a ferromagnetic material has lots of random magnetic domains that cancel eachother out however the wire makes them align to create a strong magnetic field. The stronger the current going through the wire the more powerfull the magnetic field is.

Wiring to Arduino Mega

I do not remember exactly how i wired the Electromagnet, however i have scetched out a rough schematic from my memory below. I do know that i used a hall magnet effect sensor to measure the distance from the nail to the magnet below by measuring the distance of the magnetic field.

Code

I wrote the code to be as simple as possible. It measured the strength of the below magnetic field and depending on how weak or strong the magnetic field is it increased or decreased the strength of the electromagnet. I also had to be careful because it would also measure the strenght of the electromagnet so i had to compensate for that.

Results

Electromagnet Results

I managed to get the magnet to float up and down if i kept my fingers there to block it from going too far, however I was not able to achieve my overall goal of making it freely float without any interference.

Discussion

The experiment was not a complete success because it did not float on its own. It did not work because i attatched it to a cardboard box which moved a lot, so i could not get an accurate measurement and write the code to that because it kept changing.
